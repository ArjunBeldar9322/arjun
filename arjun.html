The Reasoning of Arjuna

On the battlefield of Kurukshetra, Arjuna, the warrior prince, faces a moral dilemma. Torn between his duty as a warrior and his compassion for his relatives, teachers, and friends on the opposing side, he lowers his bow, questioning the purpose of war and the meaning of righteousness.

His reasoning is rooted in a deep ethical and philosophical conflict: Should he perform his duty (dharma) or follow his emotions? This question sets the stage for the divine discourse of the Bhagavad Gita by Lord Krishna.
